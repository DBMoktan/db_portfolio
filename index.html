The Story So Far

I am Dhana Bahadur Muktan, an IT Lecturer and Machine Learning Engineer based in Nepal, focused on designing and developing practical, data-driven solutions that bridge education, research, and real-world impact.

My professional journey began in education shortly after completing my +2, driven by a strong belief that knowledge grows through sharing. This path helped me build a solid foundation in communication, analytical thinking, and problem-solving through continuous classroom engagement. Over time, I transitioned into specialized IT education, completing my BSc in CSIT followed by an MTech in Information Technology, while actively contributing to academic teaching and mentoring.

Currently, I am focused on growing as an AI Engineer, with active interest in Machine Learning, Generative AI, Natural Language Processing (NLP), and Agentic AI systems, aiming to build intelligent and scalable AI solutions for real-world applications.

I am passionate about building impactful AI/ML systems and continuously advancing my expertise in data science, model development, and modern artificial intelligence technologies.

05 — Research

Academic Contributions

MTech Thesis

Machine Learning Approaches for Crop Yield Prediction Using Agro-Climatic Parameters: A Nepali Context

This research investigates the application of multiple supervised learning algorithms — including Random Forest, XGBoost, Support Vector Regression, and Artificial Neural Networks — to predict agricultural yields for key Nepalese crops. Using a novel dataset compiled from the Department of Agriculture (DoA) Nepal and meteorological records, the study establishes benchmark performance metrics and identifies the most effective ML approaches for Nepal's unique agro-climatic conditions.
